About Me

I am currently a student at Simon Fraser University studying Computing Science and Mathematics. On the side, I pursue freelance projects, watch birds and play the occasional video game. My interests lie with front end development and data visualization. Most of my self directed projects revolve around the design aspects of web development while others attempt to solve personal problems and/or automate certain time consuming tasks.

I adore simple, minimalistic sites and, as a result, have cultivated a minimalist style in the majority of my work. Sites, slides and papers all have a clean, attractive amount of white space accompanied with the bare necessities; design by subtraction.
Moreover, a desire for efficiency fuels my strive for organization. Projects- such as Laputan Machines -aim to formalize my cumbersome writing journal by framing my thoughts and criticisms of video games as a blog. This 'reframing' assists me in overcoming laziness by implementing a demand system into my personal routine- what was a journal is now a bow with a constant need for fresh content.
